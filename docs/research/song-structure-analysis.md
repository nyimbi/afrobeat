### Key findings

1. **Song length:** Median 33 lines, 197 words. Top artists average 39 lines — longer than corpus mean.

2. **Stanza structure:** Songs average 8.9 stanzas of 4.4 lines each. 4-line stanzas are the single most common block size.

3. **Repetition:** Corpus-wide repetition ratio is 0.25. Most repetitive genre: **amapiano** (0.29). Least: **rai** (0.20). High repetition (chorus-heavy songs) correlates with shorter average line length.

4. **Language mixing:** 100% of songs mix two or more languages. Most multilingual genre: **afropop** (100%). English is the dominant co-language in nearly all combinations.

5. **Rhyme density:** **amapiano** has the highest rhyme density (0.71). Genres with higher rhyme density tend to have shorter, more regular line lengths.

6. **Local language content:** **rai** has the lowest English function-word ratio (0.02) — most lyrically local. **afrobeats** is most English-dominant (0.21).

7. **Top-artist signature:** Compared to the full corpus, the top-15 most-catalogued artists write less repetitive songs, with more stanzas, suggesting narrative verse-heavy structures.
